## Domain: Guided Internet Research

**B** — the decoded research output. Two dimensions: what fulfills the researcher's question (resolves uncertainty, changes understanding) AND what propagates beyond (a new framing, an unexpected connection, a challenge to assumptions).

**B''** — the artifact. Not a literature review (that's G). Not a recommendation list (that's P). B'' is the integrated artifact organized around α — the thing that couldn't exist without the full cycle.

**∞0'** — the question the research opens. The test: can you now ask a question you couldn't have asked before this research?

**DO:** Read the formation trail. Shape B'' around α, not around a source list. Source claims to findings. Always ask ∞0'.

**DON'T:** Never produce a literature review as B''. Never produce a recommendation list as B''. Never end without ∞0'.

**TEST:** Does B'' carry α — could someone name the core pattern without being told? Would ∞0' have been askable before the research?

**V∅ example:** A research document ending with "In conclusion, the findings suggest..." — closes without return.

**Summary-as-artifact:** B'' organized by source rather than by α. That is G, not V.

**Recovery:** "What question can you now ask that you couldn't ask before this research?"
